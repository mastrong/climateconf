The Building

Originally designed and built in the 1950s by Pier Luigi Nervi, the pavilion is located in the northern area of Bologna. Today, it is home to initiatives focused on big data, artificial intelligence, meteorology, climate research, and innovation.

The Botte B4 Pavilion is part of the DAMA Technopole, a redeveloped industrial area formerly known as Manifattura Tabacchi. The pavilion incorporates sustainable architectural features and houses the ECMWF data centre, one of Europe's leading meteorological computing facilities.

How to Get Here

By bicycle

Via Stalingrado includes a dedicated cycle lane (Cycle Route 7).

By public transport

From Bologna city centre and Bologna Centrale (fast trains):

  • Bus line 25 to Casoni on Via Ferrarese.
    At the light, turn right onto Via Giovanni Casoni, then left onto Via Stalingrado. Walk about 20 metres to the data centre entrance.
    Line 25 route and timetable
  • Bus line 68 directly to Via Stalingrado.
    Fewer stops in the city centre.
    Line 68 route and timetable

Tickets are available via vending machines onboard buses (“biglietto ordinario”) or through the Roger and Muver mobile apps.

By car or taxi

  • From city centre: approx. 10–15 minutes (EUR 15–20).
  • From Bologna Airport: approx. 20 minutes (EUR 15–20).

Mention “ex Manifattura Tabacchi / Tecnopolo” to taxi drivers for clarity. Credit cards are widely accepted.

Parking is available on site.

Where to Stay in Bologna

For a full list of accommodation options, visit the Bologna Welcome – Where to Stay page.

We strongly recommend booking early, as Bologna remains busy with trade fairs and cultural events through autumn.

While lodging is available near the data centre in northern Bologna, most visitors choose to stay in the city centre.

  • City Centre (recommended): walkable, lively, excellent dining.
  • Near the Data Centre: quiet and convenient for daily sessions.
  • Avoid: Via Zamboni / Piazza Verdi (university zone) and the train-station/Montagnola area due to noise.
