Submissions

We invite proposals that explore how data visualization can support understanding, engagement, and meaningful action in the face of climate and environmental crises. Submissions may address how climate and environmental information is transformed into compelling visual narratives for diverse audiences including policymakers, researchers, and frontline communities.

We welcome scientific, journalistic, artistic, and design-based visualizations, as well as tools, methods, and case studies involving interdisciplinary collaboration and co-design.

Themes

  • Visual Communication
  • Narrative Visualization
  • Information Design
  • Design Process
  • Participatory Design / Co-Design
  • Diversity, Equity, Inclusiveness, Ethics
  • Open Data and Open Science
  • Education and Capacity Building
  • Climate Variability and Change
  • Weather Extremes
  • Climate Risks
  • Climate Services
  • Climate Impacts
  • Decision Making
  • Monitoring and Modelling
  • Adaptation and Climate Resilience
  • Mitigation

General Submission Guidelines

All submissions must include:

  • Title
  • Abstract or description (up to 250 words)
  • Three keywords
  • Contact information

The submission platform will allow you to select the contribution type (oral or workshop).

Submission deadline: March 31, 2026

Notification of acceptance: May 31, 2026

Oral Contributions

Format

Participants may submit proposals for oral presentations (15 minutes including questions). Accepted contributions will be assigned to thematic sessions across scientific visualization, media and news visualization, artistic and creative work, or tools and services.

Submit

Submit your oral proposal using this form.

Workshops

Format

We welcome workshops, artistic interventions, hybrid formats, collective sessions, or other interactive contributions that do not fit the oral/poster structure. Workshops are typically 90 minutes, though shorter formats may be considered.

Submit

Submit your workshop proposal using this form.

Travel support may be considered for presenters with limited access to funding, including early-career researchers, inclusiveness-target countries, or least-developed countries. Apply using this form. All participants are responsible for their own travel and accommodation.
